# Camp email types

Load this for examples of the everyday emails a camp sends. Write the final version in camp-voice, get every date and amount exact, and mark anything you do not have as a `[placeholder]`. Each email does one job and carries one action.

This skill covers everyday emails only. It does not include the hard messages — an injury, a serious incident, a behavior concern, suspected abuse, or anything with safety or legal exposure. Those are drafted by the camp's own experienced staff, following its incident protocols, accreditation guidance, and legal or PR review, and a serious incident is a phone call first. There are no templates for them here, by design.

## Enrollment and nurture

For prospective families. A short sequence, a few days apart, beats one long email.

- **Welcome / first contact (the most-opened email):** Subject "Welcome to [camp]." Body: a warm hello, the one or two facts that matter most, and a single next step (browse sessions, or book a call). Save the rest for later emails.
- **Reassurance (what parents weigh):** Subject "A first summer away, made easy." Body: speak to one worry at a time — homesickness, safety, cost — with how the camp handles it, and a link to the page that says more.
- **Early-bird:** Subject "Early-bird pricing ends [date]." Body: the rate, the deadline, one link. Let the window do the work.
- **Abandoned registration (started, did not finish):** Subject "Your spot at [camp] is still open." Body: a line that you saved their progress, the one step left, the link, a note about payment options if they exist, and a real person to reply to.

## Re-enrollment and retention

For families who just attended. Send as the session ends, while it is fresh.

- **Re-enroll (priority window):** Subject "Save [child]'s place for next summer." Body: a warm line about this summer, next year's dates, and the returning-family window or discount — their access before public registration opens.
- **Sibling:** Subject "Bring a sibling next summer." Body: the sibling discount, who it applies to, and the link.
- **Referral ask:** Subject "Know a family who'd love [camp]?" Body: a short thank-you, the referral offer, and an easy way to share. Hand the mechanics to camp-referrals.

## In-season parent updates

For families whose children are away. Short and warm; the photos and website carry the rest.

> Subject: Week three at [camp]
> A short, warm paragraph on what the week looked like, with two or three quick highlights. A note that fresh photos are up on [where], with every cabin covered. If there is one thing for families to do, it goes here, once. Photos and names only with consent.

## The season newsletter

Lead with a real moment, keep the dates and any deadline up top, keep it scannable, and include one action only if there is one.

> Subject: What's happening at [camp]
> A warm opening line. Upcoming dates and reminders, front and center. A short "what's happening" section with one or two photos (with consent). One clear action, if there is one.

## Lifecycle and operational reminders

Tied to the enrollment journey, where the facts must be exact. Double-check every date and amount.

- **Instant confirmation:** Subject "You're in — [child] is registered for [session]." Body: confirm the session and dates, what happens next, and where to find their account. Send it the moment they register or pay.
- **Welcome packet:** Subject "Getting ready for [session]." Body: the key dates, what to read now, and who to contact with questions.
- **Forms due:** Subject "[name], [child]'s forms are due [date]." Body: which forms, the link, who to ask.
- **Pre-camp instructions:** Subject "Drop-off and packing for [session]." Body: drop-off time and place, the packing list link, what to label, who to contact on day one.
- **A balance, handled kindly:** money can be awkward, so keep it warm and low-pressure, with an easy way out.

> Subject: [child]'s balance for [session]
> Dear [parent], a friendly reminder that [child]'s balance of $[amount] for [session] is due [date]. If now is not a good time, we have [installment or campership options], so just reply and we will sort it out. Thank you, [Name].
