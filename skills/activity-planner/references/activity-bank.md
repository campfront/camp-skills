# A vetted starter bank of camp games and activities

Load this for concrete games and activities to recommend. Everything here is traceable to a current, authoritative source: Playworks' live game library (a national nonprofit built around inclusive, structured play), Michigan State University Extension 4-H (a land-grant university youth-development source, articles dated 2024), and the American Camp Association's Camping Magazine (the US camp accrediting body's publication, 2025–2026). Nothing is pulled from crowd-sourced compendiums or content farms.

How to use it: this is a starting point, not a script. Pick from it using the age, energy, group, space, and gear the situation calls for (see the matching guide and the run-it sequence in `activity-selection.md`), then swap in what the camp already loves. Each entry lists a way to keep every camper in play, since the skill never runs an activity that sidelines a child for a whole round.

The bar for adding an activity here: it traces to a current Tier-1 source (or is a universal, zero-risk classic), and it is not on the excluded list at the end. Hold any new entry to that bar.

Tags read: *ages · energy · group size · setting · gear*.

## Name games and icebreakers

**Movement Name Game** — *ages 5–12 · low to medium · any size · either · no gear*
In a circle, each camper says their name with a movement, and the group repeats the name and movement around the circle. Keep everyone in: built in, every camper gets a turn and is echoed by the group. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/movement-name-game/))

**Name Touch** — *ages 8–11 · medium · any size · either · no gear*
One camper in the center calls another's name; that camper must call a different name before being tagged. Keep everyone in: a tagged camper just swaps into the center, no sit-out. Light "butterfly" tags only. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/name-touch/))

**I Love My Neighbor** (Stand Up If) — *ages 5–17 · medium · 11+ · either · chairs or cones, one fewer than players*
The center camper names a trait ("...especially anyone who likes pizza"); everyone who matches scrambles for a new spot, and whoever is left becomes the next caller. Keep everyone in: the leftover camper just calls next. Use the seated "Stand Up If" version for mobility needs, and steer callers toward likes and clothing, not appearance. For a no-competition version with no spare seats, run ACA's "Switch Places," where campers move to a new spot whenever a called-out statement fits them. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/i-love-my-neighbor/); [ACA Camping Magazine, May 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-nifty-activities-keep-campers-busy))

**Boppity Bop Bop Bop** — *ages 7–12 · medium · 10+ · either · no gear*
The center camper points at someone and says "Boppity bop bop bop"; the chosen camper must say "Bop" first. Keep everyone in: a caught camper moves to the center and stays in play. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/boppity-bop-bop-bop/))

**Hi, My Name Is** — *ages 6–17 · low to medium · any size · either · no gear*
On a signal, everyone moves around introducing themselves to as many people as possible in about a minute. Keep everyone in: no elimination; offer a wave or fist-bump instead of a handshake. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/news/back-to-school-icebreaker-games/))

**Dicebreakers** — *ages 6–17 · calm · small groups · either · dice and a set of question cards*
In small groups, campers take turns rolling a die and answering the matching question, surfacing what they have in common. Keep everyone in: everyone rolls and answers; let a camper pass on any question they would rather skip. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Sep 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/activities-encourage-belonging-among-campers))

## Big-group field games

**Sharks and Minnows** — *ages 6–12 · high · 10+ · outdoor · no gear (cones optional)*
Sharks in the middle call the minnows across a marked field; minnows sprint to the far line without being tagged. Keep everyone in: tagged minnows become sharks and keep playing. Butterfly tags only. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/sharks-and-minnows/))

**Everyone's It Tag** — *ages 5–12 · high · 10+ · either (keep the area compact) · no gear*
Everyone is "it" at once inside a defined area; tag others and keep your own tally over a short timed round. Keep everyone in: this is the no-elimination tag, nobody stops when tagged; stepping out costs tally points or jumping jacks, never a sit-out. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/everyones-it-tag/))

**Blob Tag** — *ages 6–12 · high · 10+ · outdoor · no gear*
Two campers link up as "the blob" and chase; anyone tagged links on, and at four the blob splits in two. Keep everyone in: tagged campers join the blob rather than leaving. Link hands if elbows are awkward. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/blob-tag/))

**Elbow Tag** — *ages 8–14 · medium to high · 10+ · outdoor (indoor mods given) · no gear*
Linked-elbow pairs scatter; one chaser pursues one runner, who escapes by linking onto a pair and bumping the far partner off to become the new runner. Keep everyone in: chaser and runner roles rotate continuously. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/elbow-tag/))

**Capture the Flag** — *ages 9–17 · high · 12–30 · outdoor · 2 flags, cones*
Two teams defend a flag on their half; campers cross to steal the other flag and carry it back without being tagged. Keep everyone in: a tagged camper waits briefly in a holding zone and is freed the moment a teammate tags them, so they re-enter rather than sit out the round. Butterfly tags; keep defenders a step off the flag to avoid pile-ups. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/capture-the-flag/))

**Hula Hoop Loop** — *ages 6–17 · active · two large groups (15+ each is ideal) · outdoor · one large hula hoop*
Each group joins hands in a circle and races to pass a hula hoop all the way around without letting go of hands. Keep everyone in: cooperative, no elimination, and everyone stays in the chain the whole time. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Sep 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/activities-encourage-belonging-among-campers))

**Three-Legged Race** — *ages 6–14 · active · pairs · outdoor · a soft tie (a scarf or fabric strip) per pair*
Partners stand side by side with their inside legs tied together and move to the finish as a pair. Keep everyone in: everyone races at once and everyone finishes, no elimination. Run it on grass at a brisk walk, keep the course short, and pair campers of similar height to avoid trips and falls. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Jan 2026](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/camp-activities-ring-new-year))

## Indoor and rainy-day games

**Silent Ball** — *ages 7–13 · low · 6–20 · indoor · one soft foam ball*
Campers silently pass a soft ball; anyone who talks, drops, or throws badly is out. Keep everyone in: use the "Silent Trees" variant, where a camper who errs becomes a planted "tree" and re-enters by deflecting or intercepting a pass. Foam ball only. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/silent-ball/))

**Four Corners** — *ages 6–10 · low to medium · any size · indoor · no gear*
The counter shuts their eyes and counts down while campers pick a corner, then calls a corner and those campers sit; an empty corner brings everyone back. Keep everyone in: fast re-entry is built in (empty-corner call), and rotating the counter every couple of minutes keeps "sit" to a one-round pause. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/four-corners/))

**Switch** — *ages 6–10 · medium (walk indoors) · small, rotate others in · indoor · 5 cones or a four-square court*
On "Switch!" everyone moves to a new corner, with quick rock-paper-scissors settling ties. Keep everyone in: no elimination by design; campers cycle through positions and ties resolve instantly. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/switch/))

**Charades Tag** — *ages 8–11 · medium to high · 10+ · indoor (clear the space) · no gear*
Two teams face off; one team acts a charade, the other guesses and, on a correct guess, chases them back to a safety line. Keep everyone in: a tag just switches you to the other team. Walking or light tag only indoors. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/charades-tag/))

**Huckle Buckle Beanstalk** — *ages 6–10 · low · any size · indoor · one small object*
The leader hides an object in plain sight; campers walk quietly with hands behind backs to spot it, then sit and softly say the phrase without giving it away. Keep everyone in: use the hot-and-cold variant so everyone keeps searching until all succeed; hands behind backs keeps it calm. Also good as a wind-down. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/huckle-buckle-beanstalk/))

**Pass It!** — *all ages · active · medium to large, in teams · either · two small objects (a rubber chicken, a spoon)*
Teams race to pass an object hand to hand around their circle; the first team to send it all the way round wins, then add a second object going the other way. Keep everyone in: a cooperative team relay where no one sits out (skip the hot-potato music-elimination variation). ([ACA Camping Magazine, May 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-nifty-activities-keep-campers-busy))

**Balloon in the Air!** — *all ages · active · medium to large · either · one or more balloons*
The group keeps a balloon off the floor as long as possible, raising the challenge with more balloons or hands-only then feet-only rules. Keep everyone in: cooperative, everyone keeps it up together with no elimination. Clear popped balloon pieces promptly, since balloons are a choking risk for young children. ([ACA Camping Magazine, May 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-nifty-activities-keep-campers-busy))

## Wind-down and calm games

**Line of Silence** — *ages 6–14 · low · any size · either · no gear*
Without speaking, the group lines up in a given order (birthday, height, name) using only gestures, then debriefs and runs a new category. Keep everyone in: everyone stays in every round; pick sort criteria that single nobody out, and pair a camper who needs help. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/line-of-silence/))

**Up, Down, Stop, Go** — *ages 6–12 · low to medium · any size · indoor · no gear*
Campers do the opposite of the leader's call (up means squat, stop means move, and so on). Keep everyone in: no elimination by design; a miss is a quick task, then straight back in. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/up-down-stop-go/))

**Pulse** — *ages 7–14 · low · two teams of 5–8 · either · small object, chair, coin*
Two seated lines hold hands and watch a coin; on heads, each team squeezes a pulse down the line so the end camper grabs the object. Keep everyone in: rotate positions so everyone gets the grab spot, set "gentle squeeze only," and offer a light shoulder tap instead of hand-holding. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/pulse/))

## Team-building and initiative challenges

These earn a debrief (what happened, so what, now what). Keep groups small enough that everyone contributes.

**All Tangled Up** (Human Knot) — *ages 8–14 · low to medium · 6–12 · either · no gear*
Campers grab two non-adjacent hands across a tight circle, then untangle into a single ring without letting go. Keep everyone in: cooperative, no elimination; offer a caller or observer role for anyone who would rather not be in the tangle, respect personal space, and watch for shoulder strain. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/all-tangled-up/))

**Group Juggle** — *ages 8–17 · medium · 8–20 · either · several soft throwables*
The circle sets a fixed tossing pattern, then the leader feeds in more objects so several are moving at once. Keep everyone in: cooperative, drops are expected not penalized; soft objects only, and allow underhand passes. ([MSU Extension 4-H, Dec 2024](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/easy-icebreakers-group-juggle))

**Grid Minefield** — *ages 9–17 · low to medium · 6–20 · indoor or flat outdoor · tarp, tape, a secret-path note*
One camper at a time steps square to square to find a hidden safe path; a wrong step sends them back, and the group must remember and share the route to get everyone across. Keep everyone in: restarts are individual and not punitive; keep footing stable. ([MSU Extension 4-H, Dec 2024](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/easy-icebreakers-grid-minefield))

**Spaghetti and Marshmallow Towers** — *ages 8–17 · low · teams of 3–5 · indoor · dry spaghetti, marshmallows*
Teams build the tallest free-standing tower they can in a time limit. Keep everyone in: a calm tabletop task most campers can join; keep teams small so everyone builds. Skip any "fire" stress test in a camp setting. ([MSU Extension 4-H, Nov 2024](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/easy-icebreakers-spaghetti-and-marshmallow-towers))

**Alligator Swamp** (Cross the Swamp) — *ages 8–14 · medium · 10+ or teams of 6–10 · either · a few carpet squares or sacks, cones*
The team crosses a "swamp" using fewer portable platforms than there are campers, passing them forward; touching the ground sends the group back to start. Keep everyone in: a misstep sets the whole group back, so it stays collaborative rather than exposing one camper. Non-slip squares on smooth floors. ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/alligator-swamp-2/))

**Moving Water** — *all ages · active · small teams of 3–4 · outdoor · two containers and half a sponge per team, a measuring cup*
Teams ferry water from a full container to an empty one using only a sponge, working out the most efficient method. Keep everyone in: a small-team relay where everyone carries water; reward teamwork and strategy over raw speed, as the source frames it. Run it outdoors for the spills, and watch footing on wet ground. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Jul 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-creative-activities-keep-kids-engaged))

## No-equipment, low-prep games

Plus, from above and usable anywhere with nothing: **Everyone's It Tag**, **Movement Name Game**, and **Line of Silence**.

**Rock Paper Scissors** (RoShamBo) — *ages 5–17 · low · any size, in pairs · either · no gear*
Pairs throw rock, paper, or scissors on the count. Keep everyone in: for a group version, the camper who loses a round joins the winner's cheering line and follows them, so everyone stays active (this group variant is a facilitation add-on, not in the source rules). ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/ro-sham-bo-or-rock-paper-scissors/))

**Simon Says** — *ages 5–9 · low to medium · any size · either · no gear*
Campers follow an action only when it is prefaced with "Simon says." Keep everyone in: run the active-consequence variant, where a caught camper does one round of jumping jacks or touches the ground and stays in, instead of sitting out (the standard rules eliminate; this no-sit-out framing is the variant to use). ([Playworks](https://www.playworks.org/game-library/simon-says/))

**Feather Race** — *ages 5–12 · active · individual or teams · either · one feather per camper (optional hoops as goals)*
Campers move a feather across the space using only their breath or a fan, no hands. Keep everyone in: everyone has their own feather and plays the whole time, no elimination. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Nov 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/camp-activities-fall))

## Crafts and maker challenges

Not every activity is a running-around game. These are calm-to-moderate maker activities for a rainy afternoon, a hot midday, or a settle-down block, and they include everyone by design, since each camper builds their own.

**Paper Plate Aquarium** — *all ages · calm · individual or small groups · either · paper plates, construction paper, glue or tape, coloring supplies (optional: shells, sand, gems)*
Campers cut and decorate a paper plate into an underwater scene with paper sea creatures. Keep everyone in: everyone makes their own, no winners; young campers use child-safe scissors. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Jul 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-creative-activities-keep-kids-engaged))

**Foil Boat Challenge** — *all ages · moderate · individual or partners · either (needs a tub of water) · aluminum foil, washers or coins, a pan or tub of water*
Campers shape a foil boat and test how much weight it carries before it sinks. Keep everyone in: judge on creativity and capacity so there is more than one way to do well, not a single winner. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Jul 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/three-creative-activities-keep-kids-engaged))

**Friendship Butterflies** — *ages 6–13 · calm · pairs or small groups · indoor · butterfly templates, construction paper strips, glue, markers*
Each camper colors half a butterfly, swaps with a partner to color the other half, then wears the finished butterfly as a paper wristband. Keep everyone in: a partner craft built around belonging; pair campers intentionally so no one is left without a partner. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Sep 2025](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/activities-encourage-belonging-among-campers))

**Milk Fireworks** — *ages 5–12 (younger need a hand) · calm, watch-and-react · individual or small groups · either · whole milk, food coloring, dish soap, a cotton swab, a shallow dish*
Campers drop food coloring into a dish of milk, then touch a soap-dipped swab to the surface and watch the colors burst and swirl. Keep everyone in: everyone runs their own dish, a calm science moment with no winners. Food coloring stains, so cover clothes and surfaces. ([ACA Camping Magazine, Jan 2026](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/camp-activities-ring-new-year))

## Nature and outdoor exploration

Quiet, inclusive ways to get a group looking closely at the outdoors. Low-risk and tool-free, good for a hike, a nature block, or a calm outdoor stretch.

**Leaf Scavenger Hunt** — *ages 5–13 · moderate · pairs or small groups · outdoor · a simple list, optional guidebooks*
Pairs search for leaves matching given traits (a serrated edge, a leaf bigger than a hand). Keep everyone in: pairs work together with no losing; make the list findable for the youngest. ([ACA Camping Magazine, May 2026](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/seven-tree-mendous-activities-promote-appreciation-forests))

**Bark and Leaf Rubbings** — *ages 5–12 · calm · individual or small groups · outdoor · unwrapped crayons, paper*
Campers lay paper over bark or a leaf and rub with the side of a crayon to lift the texture. Keep everyone in: everyone makes their own, no skill barrier. ([ACA Camping Magazine, May 2026](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/seven-tree-mendous-activities-promote-appreciation-forests))

**Adopt a Tree** — *ages 6–14 · calm · individual · outdoor · a journal, optional camera*
Each camper picks one tree to name, identify, photograph, and check on across the session. Keep everyone in: everyone has their own tree, a low-pressure ongoing activity that suits a quiet camper as well as a sociable one. ([ACA Camping Magazine, May 2026](https://www.acacamps.org/article/camping-magazine/seven-tree-mendous-activities-promote-appreciation-forests))

## Excluded by design: games not to reach for

Some traditional camp and playground games have been pulled or rewritten by modern youth-safety guidance. Do not add these to the bank or recommend them, even if a camp asks by name; offer a vetted alternative above instead.

**Out for safety or liability:**
- **Dodgeball and any "human target" game** (bombardment, prison ball, and the like). SHAPE America (the US physical-education standards body) holds these inappropriate for children; the goal is to hit other kids, and the least-skilled are out first and most.
- **Red Rover** (arm-linking and charging): broken bones and a documented negligent-supervision settlement.
- **British Bulldog / Bullrush** and other tackle-or-force-through games: fractures and concussions; removed by many schools.
- **Crack the Whip / Snap the Whip:** the whipped end of the chain gets flung off.
- **Gang-tackle games** (the game often called "kill the carrier"): uncontrolled pile-ons.
- **Trampolines** as an activity: the American Academy of Pediatrics says they should never be used in school PE.

**Out as written, fix the mechanic:**
- **Elimination games that sideline a camper for a whole round.** Use the re-entry variants baked into the games above instead.
- **Picking teams by captains.** Count off, draw cards, or pre-assign, so nobody is chosen last.
- **Forced solo performance** that puts one camper on the spot.

**Rename before running:**
- **"Chinese Whispers"** is **Telephone**.
- **Finger-gun or "pistol" poses** (as in some rock-paper-scissors triads): swap to a non-weapon set. Zero-tolerance discipline has suspended children over finger-gun gestures.
- Drop any game whose name carries a slur or a racial or cultural caricature ("Cowboys and Indians" framing, "Smear the Queer," and similar). Use a plain descriptive name.
