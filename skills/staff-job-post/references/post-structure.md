# Camp job-post template and checklists

Load this when writing a specific post. It has the section template with a counselor example, the screening-and-certifications checklist, the inclusive-language list, and where to post. Write in camp-voice, and mark any unconfirmed pay or dates as `[placeholder]`.

## The section template

Order the post like this:

1. **The camp and the role, in a line or two:** who the camp is, the role, where, and the dates.
2. **What you'll do:** the real day to day, four to six concrete lines.
3. **What you'll gain:** the career-building skills, the team and belonging, the impact on kids. For international applicants, the cultural exchange and travel.
4. **What we need from you:** the one thing they must be great at, plus the real requirements (age, certifications) framed as what they enable, and the screening and training note.
5. **The practical terms:** dates, pay, room and board, time off, transport, and whether the role is open to international (J-1) applicants.
6. **How to apply:** one clear step, with a short equal-opportunity line.

## A counselor example (overnight camp)

**Cabin Counselor at [Camp], [region], summer [year]**

> [Camp] is a [type] overnight camp on [lake or place]. We are hiring cabin counselors for summer [year], [start to end dates], living on site.

**What you'll do**
> - Live with a cabin of [age] campers and a co-counselor, and be the person they count on all day.
> - Lead and join daily activities, from [activity] to [activity].
> - Keep your campers safe, included, and busy, and help the homesick ones settle.
> - Take part in evening programs and all-camp traditions.

**What you'll gain**
> A summer that builds leadership and judgment, the kind colleges and employers notice, a close team, and the knowledge that you mattered to a kid. [For international applicants: a summer in [region] on a J-1 visa, with [agency] handling the paperwork.]

**What we need from you**
> - At least [age] by the first day of camp.
> - Comfortable keeping a group of [n] [age]-year-olds safe and busy. No prior camp experience needed.
> - Standard First Aid and CPR, or willing to certify before camp. [Lifeguard certification for waterfront roles.]
> - Able to pass our screening (an application, interview, references, and a criminal background check) and complete staff training before campers arrive.

**The practical terms**
> [Dates.] Pay [placeholder]. Room and board included. [Time off.] [Airport pickup for international staff.]

**How to apply**
> [One step: a link or email.] [Camp] is an equal-opportunity employer and welcomes applicants of all backgrounds.

## Screening and certifications to state (from ACA and OCA)

State the ones that apply to the role. They are requirements, and stating them is also a trust signal.

- The minimum age for the role.
- Standard First Aid and CPR for most roles, and lifeguard certification for waterfront roles.
- That every hire completes the camp's screening before starting: an application, interview, reference checks, and a criminal background check, plus a sex-offender-registry check and a disclosure statement for anyone working with children.
- That all staff complete mandatory training before campers arrive (child-abuse prevention and reporting, supervision, behavior management, emergencies).

Tailor these to the role, and confirm the exact rules for the camp's jurisdiction (ACA, the provincial association, and state or provincial regulation).

## Inclusive and lawful language

Keep the ad open to everyone qualified.

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| young, energetic | motivated, enthusiastic |
| native English speaker | fluent in English (only if the role needs it) |
| rockstar, ninja, guru | the real role name |
| "fast-paced" with no detail | the real demand (long days, living on site) |

- List only essential, lawful requirements, and do not require prior experience by default.
- Include an equal-opportunity line, for example: "[Camp] is an equal-opportunity employer and welcomes applicants of all backgrounds."
- Avoid language tied to age, gender, race, religion, or national origin, unless it is a lawful bona-fide requirement, and have local law checked.

## Where to post

- The ACA job board (for accredited camps) and camp-specific job boards.
- College career centers and job fairs.
- General boards such as Indeed and CoolWorks.
- J-1 placement agencies, if the camp hires international staff.
