# Camp social calendar and post ideas

Load this when planning the mix or a calendar, or writing specific posts. It has the content pillars, the season-by-season rhythm, post types with caption examples, the Reel shape, and the consent check. Write captions in camp-voice, and treat every photo of a camper as needing consent.

## Content pillars (the mix)

Pick about five recurring post types so posting is repeatable rather than invented each time. A rough split for a camp:

| Pillar | Share | What it is |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Camp life | 35% | real photos and short video of a normal day |
| People and traditions | 20% | a camper or staff spotlight, a tradition, a throwback |
| Reassurance for parents | 15% | a day in the life, homesickness, packing |
| Proof | 10% | a real parent quote or a family's story, with permission |
| Behind the scenes | 10% | off-season prep, staff training, the site |
| Announcements | 10% | enrollment open, early-bird, an open house |

## The posting rhythm by season

Move with the camp year (see camp-overview):

- **In season (summer):** a short recap a few times a week of real moments. Keep these public posts separate from any private daily photo feed for enrolled families.
- **Late summer:** closing highlights, and the first soft "next summer" note while the summer is still fresh.
- **Fall:** behind-the-scenes, alumni posts, and the enrollment-open announcement as parents start researching.
- **December to April:** the main decision stretch, so run the highest-intent content here (testimonials, a day in the life, program highlights, the early-bird deadline).
- **Spring:** staff introductions, packing and prep, a last call before sessions fill.

A sustainable baseline for a small camp is two or three posts a week plus Stories, with more in season and through the December-to-April window.

## Post types, with caption examples

Write these in camp-voice. Lead with the moment, keep it short, and add a next step only where it fits. Examples use `[placeholders]` for real details.

**Camp life:**
> First swim of the session this morning. The water was freezing and nobody cared. [photo: campers heading down to the dock]

**Camper or staff spotlight (with consent):**
> Meet [name], back for her [n]th summer and now a [role]. She runs [activity] and had half the cabin [doing the skill] by Wednesday. [photo, with consent]

**Reassurance for parents:**
> Nervous about the first night away? Here is what actually happens. [the cabin settle-in routine, two or three plain sentences]. The counselors are trained for it.

**Proof (parent quote card):**
> "[A specific, real parent quote, used with permission, attributed plainly, for example a returning family since [year].]"

**Behind the scenes:**
> The cabins are getting their summer once-over this week. [what is happening: docks back in, the climbing wall inspected]. [n] weeks until campers arrive.

**Announcement (use sparingly):**
> Summer [year] enrollment is open. [Session lengths and ages.] Early-bird rate ends [date]. Dates and enrollment are in the link in our bio.

## Reels and short video

- 15 to 30 seconds of real camp moments, shot vertical.
- Captions on, since most parents watch with the sound off.
- Put the hook in the first three seconds: a kid jumping off the dock, the first-night cabin going quiet.
- One idea per clip: a day in the life, a single activity, a camper reaction.
- Get consent for any child on camera.

## Before you post: the consent and safety check

Run this on every post that shows a child:

- [ ] A signed photo and media release covers this child.
- [ ] This child is not on the opt-out list.
- [ ] No identifying details (a full name together with the cabin, schedule, or location).
- [ ] The caption is true, with no invented quote or number.
- [ ] The caption has been run through camp-voice's avoid-list.
