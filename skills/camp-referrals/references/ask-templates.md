# Camp referral and review asks

Load this for the actual wording of a review request, a referral offer, or an ambassador note. Write the final version in camp-voice, and keep the FTC rule in mind: you can ask for, and even thank someone for, a review, but you can never tie a reward to a positive one. camp-emails sends these; this gives the compliant shapes. Mark anything you do not have as a `[placeholder]`.

## Review request (FTC-safe)

Send to recent happy families, at the moment of goodwill, with a direct link. Ask for an honest review, not a positive one.

Example, end of session:
> Hi [name], we loved having [child] at camp this summer. If you have a couple of minutes, an honest review on Google helps other families find us. Here is the link: [link]. Thank you either way.

Why it is safe: it asks for an honest review, ties no reward to the sentiment, and thanks them regardless. Never write "leave us a 5-star review and get [reward]."

If you offer a small thank-you for writing any review (this is allowed), keep it unconditional and say so:
> As a thank-you for taking the time to review us, honestly, we will add $[X] of camp-store credit to your account.

The credit is for writing a review, not for a positive one, and the wording has to make that clear.

## Bring-a-friend or referral offer

Reward the enrollment, not the review, so the new family and the referring family both benefit.

> Know a family who would love camp? Refer them, and when they enroll for a [session], you both get $[X] off. Share your link: [link].

## Sibling offer

> Bringing a sibling this summer? Each additional child from your family gets [sibling discount] off their session.

## Ambassador note (to a parent who already refers)

Keep it personal and low-key.

> Hi [name], the families you have sent us keep becoming some of our best campers, and we wanted to say thank you. If it is ever easy to share, here is a link friends can use: [link]. No pressure, and thank you for spreading the word.

## Quick FTC check on any ask

- [ ] It asks for an honest review, never a positive one.
- [ ] No reward is conditioned on the sentiment or the star rating.
- [ ] Any incentive for writing a review is unconditional and disclosed.
- [ ] Reviews by staff, the owner, or their family are disclosed as such.
- [ ] No language about screening out or hiding unhappy families.
