# Camp ad copy and specs

Load this when writing the actual ads. It has the camp ad angles, the character limits for Google and Meta, and copy templates with examples. Write every line in camp-voice, fit the platform limits, and make only claims the camp can back. Mark any price, date, or number you do not have as a `[placeholder]`.

The character limits below are current as of writing, but the platforms change them and add new formats. Treat the counts here as a guide, and confirm the current limit in the platform's own ad builder before you finalize. The builder enforces the live limit as you type, so it is the reliable check.

## Pick the angles first

Before writing headlines, choose three to five distinct angles, each a different reason a parent would click. Reworded versions of one idea do not count as a test. Angles that tend to work for camps:

- First-timer reassurance ("First summer away? Counselors trained to help them settle.")
- A specific program or age ("Soccer day camp for ages 6 to 10 in [town].")
- Local ("A [town] summer camp, ten minutes from home.")
- The deadline or offer ("Early-bird rate ends [date].")
- What makes the camp itself (non-competitive, the arts, the lake, the years running)
- The format fit (day camp as reliable summer childcare, overnight as independence)

## Google Search ads

Google assembles a few of your headlines with a description, so each piece has to stand alone and combine sensibly with the others.

Limits:
- Headlines: up to 15, each 30 characters or fewer.
- Descriptions: up to 4, each 90 characters or fewer.
- Two path fields, 15 characters each.

Among the 15 headlines, cover: at least one with the main keyword ("Summer Camp in [Town]"), one with a benefit ("Trained, Vetted Counselors"), one with the season or offer ("2026 Sessions Open Now"), and two or three calls to action ("See Dates and Enroll").

Set up alongside the ad:
- Tight ad groups by intent, one per program or age where it matters, each pointed at the matching page.
- Negative keywords so you do not pay for the wrong searches (for example "jobs," "free," or a brand you are not).
- Sitelinks and callouts (accreditation, ages, dates, "financial aid available").

Example headlines for a Denver soccer day camp (count the characters before use):
- "Soccer Day Camp in Denver" (25)
- "Ages 6 to 12, Summer 2026" (25)
- "Trained, Vetted Counselors" (26)
- "See Dates and Enroll Today" (26)

Example description:
- "A week of small-group soccer with trained coaches in central Denver, ages 6 to 12." (82)

## Meta ads (Facebook and Instagram)

Parents scroll fast, so the image or video and the first line do most of the work.

Limits and shape:
- Primary text: about 125 characters show before the "more" cut-off, so front-load the hook.
- Headline: keep to about 40 characters.
- Description: about 30 characters, and often not shown.

Lead the primary text with the hook, not the camp name. Pair it with a short video or a real photo (below), and one clear call to action.

Example, retargeting parents who viewed a session:
- Primary text: "Still deciding on summer? Our two-week session on the lake has room for ages 8 to 14. [confirm any spot count before using one.]"
- Headline: "Camp Wildwood, ages 8 to 14"
- Button: the closest platform option, such as "Learn More" or "Sign Up"

## The creative (image and video)

- Short video, 15 to 30 seconds, of real kids doing real activities at this camp. It beats anything polished.
- Captions on, since most parents watch with the sound off.
- Vertical for Stories and Reels, square for the feed.
- Put the hook in the first three seconds: a kid jumping off the dock, a quiet first night in the cabin.
- Images: real faces from this camp, not stock, with short readable text.
- Get consent before using any child's image, name, or words.

## Calls to action

Match the button to how ready the parent is: "See session dates," "Check availability," "Request the brochure," "Book a tour," "Start your application." On Meta, choose the closest platform button.

## Before you ship

- Every headline and description fits its limit. Count the characters.
- Each ad points to the matching landing page (see camp-website-copy).
- No fake urgency, no invented numbers, no child's image without consent.
- Run the copy through camp-voice's avoid-list and self-audit.
